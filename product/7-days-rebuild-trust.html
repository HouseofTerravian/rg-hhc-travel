Before You Begin

You picked this up because something broke — or bent further than it should have. Maybe it was a lie. Maybe it was a pattern. Maybe it was a slow drift away from each other that finally reached a point where you couldn't ignore it anymore.

Rebuilding trust isn't a conversation — it's a practice. It happens in the small, repeated acts of showing up, being honest when you could have hidden, and keeping your word when no one was checking. This week you start that practice. Together.

"Trust isn't something you restore in one apology. It's something you earn back, day by day, with consistency."

Each day takes about 15–25 minutes. Some of it will be uncomfortable. That discomfort is the work. Both partners need to be fully present — no deflecting, no scorekeeping, no proving. Just showing up honestly.

How To Use This Guide

1

Do this together. Neither partner does this alone. Both read, both respond, both reflect.

2

Keep it private. What's shared in these exercises stays between you. This is a closed container.

3

No debating, no defending. When your partner shares, your job is to receive it — not refute it.

4

Write your answers. The act of writing slows you down and makes things real in a way that talking alone doesn't.

5

Do the days in order. Each one builds on the last. Jumping ahead won't work.

Day One
🗺️
Map the Breach
You can't rebuild what you haven't honestly named.
Today's Mission

Each partner takes a turn answering one question: What, specifically, broke your trust? Not the full story — just the core of it, in plain language. The other partner listens without reacting, without correcting, without explaining. When both have spoken, each partner answers a second question: What do you need to feel safe again? Write the answers down. Keep them.

⏱ Allow 20–25 minutes · No phones · Sit facing each other
Why this matters: You can't navigate toward trust without first agreeing on where you are. Naming the breach clearly — without minimizing or dramatizing it — is the first act of honesty this week requires. Both of you have to be willing to look directly at it.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"The moment my trust broke — or started to — was when..."

💬

"What hurt most about it wasn't the act itself — it was..."

💬

"What I need from you to begin feeling safe is..."

Reflection — Write It Down
What was hardest to say? What surprised you about hearing your partner?

Day Two
📋
New Agreements
The old rules didn't hold. You need new ones — ones you both actually made.
Today's Mission

Together, write three to five new agreements for your relationship — specific, behavioral, and mutual. Not aspirations. Actual behaviors: what you will do, when, and how. "We will tell each other about conversations that feel risky before they happen." "I will not discuss our problems with my friends without telling you first." These don't have to be permanent — they're starting agreements. Sign them. Date them.

⏱ Allow 20–25 minutes · Write them down by hand · Both sign
Why this matters: Trust without structure is just hope. Agreements give you both a shared standard to hold each other to — and hold yourselves to. The act of writing them together makes them real. The act of signing them makes them serious.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"One thing I need you to consistently do differently going forward is..."

💬

"One thing I'm willing to do differently — because I know it matters to you — is..."

💬

"A boundary I need us both to respect from this point forward is..."

Reflection — Write It Down
Which agreement felt most important to you? Which felt hardest to commit to?

Day Three
🔁
Consistency Practice
One promise kept today is worth more than ten made yesterday.
Today's Mission

Each partner chooses one small, specific promise to keep today — something that takes under 30 minutes and can be verified. Not a grand gesture. A small, keepable act: "I will text you when I arrive." "I will put my phone away at dinner tonight." "I will follow through on the thing I said I'd handle." At the end of the day, check in: Did you do it? How did it feel? What did it mean to your partner that you did?

⏱ Choose your promise in the morning · Check in at the end of the day
Why this matters: Consistency is the only currency trust accepts. You can't buy it back with apologies — you have to earn it back with repeated, reliable action over time. Today is day one of that practice. The promise doesn't have to be large. It has to be kept.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"The small thing I chose to do today, and why I chose it, was..."

💬

"When you followed through on what you said, the way it made me feel was..."

💬

"A pattern I've noticed in myself around keeping promises is..."

Reflection — Write It Down
What did today's small act reveal about what trust actually looks like for your partner?

Day Four
🔓
Transparency
Say the thing you'd normally keep to yourself — before you're asked.
Today's Mission

Each partner shares one thing they've been keeping from the other — not a confession, but a transparency. Something you've been thinking, feeling, or doing that your partner doesn't know about and probably should. It could be small: "I've been more anxious about us than I've let on." "I talked to my sister about this and I said some things I shouldn't have." The goal isn't drama. It's the practice of offering the truth before it's demanded.

⏱ Allow 20 minutes · Share first, discuss after · No interrogating
Why this matters: Trust dies in secrecy — not always from big secrets, but from the habit of keeping things. Proactive transparency is one of the most powerful signals you can send your partner: I'm not hiding. You don't have to wonder about me. That signal, repeated, is how trust comes back.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"Something I've been keeping to myself that you deserve to know is..."

💬

"The reason I didn't say it sooner was..."

💬

"What I want you to know about where I am right now — honestly — is..."

Reflection — Write It Down
What did it feel like to share that? What did it feel like to receive it?

Day Five
✅
Verification Rituals
Checking in isn't surveillance. Done right, it's care.
Today's Mission

Together, design two or three daily check-in rituals — specific, brief, and mutual. Not "we'll just talk more." Actual rituals: a morning text that says where you'll be, a five-minute end-of-day share ("Today I felt..."), a weekly review of the agreements you made on Day 2. These rituals aren't proof of distrust. They're the scaffolding trust grows on while it's still fragile. Write them down, agree on them, and start one today.

⏱ Allow 15–20 minutes · Make them realistic — you'll actually have to do these
Why this matters: Anxiety about a partner's behavior often comes from ambiguity — not knowing, having to wonder, filling in the blanks. Rituals remove the ambiguity. They're not about control. They're about making reassurance a routine rather than a crisis response.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"The check-in that would help me feel most settled is..."

💬

"A check-in I'm willing to do consistently — because I know it matters to you — is..."

💬

"What I want these rituals to feel like — not controlling, but more like..."

Reflection — Write It Down
Which ritual will you start today? What would it mean if you kept it for 30 days straight?

Day Six
🏠
Safety Mapping
What does feeling safe with you actually look like? Map it together.
Today's Mission

Each partner answers two questions — separately, in writing, then share. First: What makes me feel emotionally safe with you? List three to five specific things. Second: What makes me feel unsafe or on-guard? Again, specific — not character attacks, but behaviors and situations. Share your lists without defensiveness. Together, circle the overlapping themes. These become your trust map: what to keep doing, and what to stop.

⏱ Allow 20–25 minutes · Write separately first · Share without defending
Why this matters: Most partners have never explicitly told each other what safety feels like. They assume the other person knows — and then feel betrayed when it isn't delivered. Safety mapping takes the guesswork out. You can't create a feeling your partner hasn't described to you.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"I feel most safe with you when you..."

💬

"I start to feel guarded when..."

💬

"What I most need you to understand about my sense of safety is..."

Reflection — Write It Down
What on your partner's list surprised you? What will you do differently starting tomorrow?

Day Seven
🕯️
The Trust Ritual
A deliberate, shared act of choosing to believe in each other again.
Today's Mission

Create a trust ritual you'll repeat weekly — a brief, intentional act that symbolizes your commitment to rebuilding. It could be reading your Day 2 agreements together. It could be a short check-in over tea or coffee: "One thing that made me feel trusted this week... One thing I want to do better..." It doesn't need to be long. It needs to be kept. Do the ritual tonight for the first time. Schedule it for the next four weeks.

⏱ Allow 20 minutes tonight · Schedule the next four occurrences · Keep them
Why this matters: Trust rebuilt without maintenance erodes again. The weekly ritual is how you protect what you've built this week — not by revisiting the wound, but by reaffirming the commitment. Something small, done together, on purpose, every week. That's what lasting trust actually looks like.
