RG · Relationship Goals Introduction

Welcome

This guide is for couples who aren't in crisis — but who refuse to settle for "fine." Your marriage works. But you know it could be extraordinary.

Over the next seven days, you'll have structured conversations that go deeper than your daily routine allows. Each exercise takes 20–30 minutes and is designed to be done together, with honesty and intention.

"There are no wrong answers. There is only showing up."

Set aside time each day — morning, evening, whenever you can both be fully present. Phones down. Defenses lowered. Hearts open.

How To Use This Guide

1

Set a time together. Morning coffee, after dinner, before bed — pick a time you can both commit to each day.

2

Read the exercise out loud. One partner reads the day's exercise to the other. This small act sets the tone.

3

Do the exercise together. Follow the prompts. Take your time. There's no rushing this.

4

Use the reflection space. Write down what came up for you — individually or together. These notes become your story.

5

Don't skip days. The sequence matters. Each day builds on the last.

Day One
💍
The State of Us
Before you can strengthen your marriage, you need to see it clearly.
Today's Exercise

Before you can strengthen your marriage, you need to see it clearly — not the version you present to the world, but the real, daily texture of your partnership. Today you each take an honest inventory of where things stand.

⏱ Allow 20 minutes · No phones · Side by side or face to face
Why this matters: Honesty about where you are is the first act of building something better.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"On a scale of 1–10, how strong does our marriage feel to you right now? Explain the number."

💬

"What is the best thing about us right now — the thing that works even when other things don't?"

💬

"What is one area where you feel we've been coasting instead of growing?"

Reflection — Write It Down
What surprised you about your partner's answers? What do you agree on?

What Made Us
Return to the beginning and remember why you chose each other.
Today's Exercise

Every marriage has an origin story — the moments that built it. Today you return to the beginning and remember why you chose each other. Not with nostalgia that avoids the present, but with gratitude that fuels the future.

⏱ Allow 25 minutes · Be specific — details bring memories to life

💬

💬

"What quality in me surprised you — something you didn't expect when we first met?"

💬

"What from our early days do you want to recapture?"

Day Three

The Words You Need to Hear
Name the words you most need — and say the ones your partner most needs.
Today's Exercise

Most people walk around with unmet needs they've stopped asking for. In a strong marriage, partners learn to ask clearly and give generously. Today you name the words you most need to hear — and you say the words your partner most needs.

⏱ Allow 20 minutes · No phones · Eye contact when sharing
Why this matters: The words that strengthen a marriage are often the ones that feel almost too vulnerable to say.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"The words I most need to hear from you right now are..."

💬

"Something I wish I said to you more often is..."

💬

"One compliment I've been thinking but haven't said out loud is..."

Reflection — Write It Down
What felt hardest to ask for? What felt best to finally say?

Day Four
🔍
Your Unspoken Expectations
Surface the invisible expectations that shape — and strain — your marriage.
Today's Exercise

Every marriage carries invisible expectations — about roles, responsibilities, affection, ambition, parenting, money, sex. Many conflicts are actually collisions between expectations that were never spoken. Today you surface them.

⏱ Allow 30 minutes · Be honest — kindly, but honestly
Why this matters: An expectation that stays unspoken becomes a resentment waiting to happen.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"One expectation I have of you that I've never clearly stated is..."

💬

"One expectation I think you have of me that feels heavy or unclear is..."

💬

"How can we handle unspoken expectations better going forward?"

Reflection — Write It Down
Which expectation surprised you most? What will you do differently now that it's spoken?

The Dream Within the Marriage
Excavate the shared dream buried under daily life.
Today's Exercise

Every partner holds a dream for what the marriage could become — a vision of the life you want to build together. Sometimes those dreams get buried under logistics, routines, and responsibilities. Today you excavate them.

⏱ Allow 25 minutes · Dream out loud — this is allowed
Why this matters: A marriage without a shared dream is a partnership running on maintenance. Dream together.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"In five years, what does our marriage look like at its absolute best?"

💬

"What shared dream have we stopped talking about?"

💬

"What is one bold thing we could do together this year that would make us feel alive?"

Reflection — Write It Down
Where did your dreams overlap? What does that tell you about what you both want?

Day Six
🛠️
The Repair That's Overdue
Name the unresolved wound. Not to re-litigate. To heal.
Today's Exercise

Most marriages carry at least one unresolved wound — something that happened, was never fully addressed, and now sits between you like furniture you walk around. Today you name it. Not to re-litigate. To heal.

⏱ Allow 30 minutes · No interruptions · Listen before responding
Why this matters: The repair you've been avoiding is often the one that would change everything.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"Something between us that I don't think we ever fully resolved is..."

💬

"What I needed from you at that time — and maybe still need — is..."

💬

"What would 'resolved' look like to you? What would have to happen?"

Reflection — Write It Down
What did it feel like to finally name it? What does repair look like from here?
