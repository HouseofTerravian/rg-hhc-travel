RG · Relationship Goals Introduction

Before You Begin

You picked this up for a reason. Maybe things have felt distant lately. Maybe there's been tension you can't quite name. Maybe you just want to feel closer to the person you chose — and you're not sure how to get back there.

This guide doesn't ask you to be perfect. It doesn't ask you to have all the answers. It asks for seven days of showing up — honestly, openly, together.

"The strongest relationships aren't built in grand gestures. They're built in the small, consistent, daily acts of choosing each other."

Each day takes about 10–20 minutes. There are no right or wrong answers. The only rule is that both partners participate — fully present, phones down, defenses lowered.

How To Use This Guide

1

Set a time together. Morning coffee, after dinner, before bed — pick a time you can both commit to each day.

2

Read the mission out loud. One partner reads the day's mission to the other. This small act sets the tone.

3

Do the exercise together. Follow the prompts. Take your time. There's no rushing this.

4

Use the reflection space. Write down what came up for you — individually or together. These notes become your story.

5

Don't skip days. The sequence matters. Each day builds on the last.

Day One
🚪
Open the Door
Share something you've been holding back — even just a little.
Today's Mission

Each partner takes turns sharing one thing they've been holding inside — something they haven't said out loud lately. It could be a fear, a hope, a frustration, or simply something they've been meaning to say. The other partner listens without responding, fixing, or defending. Just listens.

⏱ Allow 10–15 minutes · No phones · Side by side or face to face
Why this matters: Most disconnection doesn't happen in one big moment. It builds quietly — through things left unsaid, feelings quietly swallowed, and conversations that never quite happened. Today you open the door back to each other.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"Something I've wanted to say to you lately but haven't is..."

💬

"One thing I've been afraid to bring up because I didn't want to upset you is..."

💬

"Something I need more of from you right now is..."

Reflection — Write It Down
How did it feel to say that out loud? What surprised you?

Day Two
🙏
The Gratitude Exchange
Say the things you feel but rarely say.
Today's Mission

Each partner writes down — or speaks aloud — five specific things they genuinely appreciate about the other. Not general praise. Specific. "I appreciate that you checked on me after my tough day last Tuesday." "I love the way you laugh when something really catches you off guard." Specificity is what makes gratitude land.

⏱ Allow 15–20 minutes · Writing encouraged · Eye contact when sharing
Why this matters: We often feel gratitude and never say it. Over time, the unsaid appreciation becomes invisible — and people start feeling taken for granted. Today you make the invisible visible.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"Something you do that I don't thank you enough for is..."

💬

"A quality in you that I genuinely admire — and don't say often enough — is..."

💬

"One moment recently where you showed up for me in a way I won't forget is..."

Reflection — Write It Down
What felt hardest to say? What felt best to hear?

Day Three
👂
The Listen
Speak to be understood. Listen to understand — not to respond.
Today's Mission

Partner A speaks for 5 uninterrupted minutes about how they've been feeling in the relationship lately — the good, the hard, the in-between. Partner B's only job is to listen. No fixing. No defending. No "but." When Partner A finishes, Partner B reflects back what they heard: "What I heard you say is..." Then switch. After both have spoken, discuss what landed.

⏱ Allow 20–25 minutes · No interruptions · Set a timer if needed
Why this matters: Most couples aren't failing at talking. They're failing at listening. Real listening — without forming your rebuttal — is one of the rarest and most loving things you can offer another person.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"Lately I've been feeling... and I don't think you know it."

💬

"When I try to talk to you about hard things, I sometimes feel..."

💬

"What I wish you understood about my experience right now is..."

Reflection — Write It Down
What did you hear that you hadn't fully understood before?

Day Four
🌉
The Memory Bridge
Go back to a moment when everything felt right between you.
Today's Mission

Each partner shares one specific memory from your relationship — a moment when you felt most connected, most loved, most like yourselves together. Describe it in detail: where you were, what was said, what you felt. Then together, identify what was present in that moment that you want more of now.

⏱ Allow 15–20 minutes · Be specific — details bring memories to life
Why this matters: Your history together is a resource. When things feel strained, remembering who you've been for each other is a compass back. The memory bridge isn't about living in the past — it's about reclaiming what's already yours.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"A moment when I felt closest to you was... and here's why..."

💬

"When I remember that time, what I miss most is..."

💬

"If we could bring one thing from that moment into our relationship today, it would be..."

Reflection — Write It Down
What does this memory remind you both are capable of?

Day Five
🔍
The Honest Check-In
Where are we, really? This is the conversation most couples avoid.
Today's Mission

Both partners rate the current state of the relationship in four areas — on a scale of 1 to 10 — and share their number and the reason behind it. The four areas: Connection, Trust, Communication, and Fun. After sharing scores, discuss one area you both want to improve — and one small action each of you will take this week.

⏱ Allow 20–25 minutes · Be honest — kindly, but honestly
Why this matters: You can't fix what you won't name. This exercise gives both partners a shared map of where you are. Not to assign blame — but to get honest about the work ahead and commit to it together.
Rate Your Relationship (1–10) & Share Why
🔗

Connection: How emotionally close do we feel right now?

🤝

Trust: How safe do we feel being fully honest with each other?

💬

Communication: How well are we actually hearing each other?

😄

Fun: Are we still enjoying each other's company?

Reflection — Write It Down
Which area needs the most attention? What's one thing each of you will do about it?

Day Six
🔭
The Vision
What do you both want this relationship to look and feel like?
Today's Mission

Each partner describes their vision for the relationship — not as a complaint about now, but as a genuine picture of what they want. Where do you see yourselves in one year? What does the relationship feel like at its best? What are you doing together, how are you treating each other, what has changed? Share your visions, then look for the overlap — and name it out loud.

⏱ Allow 20 minutes · Dream out loud — this is allowed
Why this matters: Couples who share a vision have somewhere to go together. Without a shared picture of "better," both partners are navigating separately. Today you build the same map.
Conversation Prompts
💬

"In one year, I want us to feel... and look like..."

💬

"Something I want us to build or experience together that we haven't yet is..."

💬

"The version of us I'm working toward looks like..."

Reflection — Write It Down
Where did your visions overlap? What does that tell you about what you're both building?

Day Seven
💍
The Recommitment
A simple, personal promise — made right now, to each other.
Today's Mission

Each partner writes — by hand if possible — a short personal promise to the other. Not a wedding vow. Not a grand speech. Just one genuine, specific commitment for the weeks ahead. Something you will do differently. Something you will keep doing. Something you will stop doing. Read them to each other. Then keep them somewhere you'll both see.

⏱ Allow 20–25 minutes · Write first, then read aloud · Keep the notes
Why this matters: Seven days of honest conversation means nothing without a step forward. This is that step. A recommitment isn't about the past — it's about who you're choosing to be for each other starting today.
Prompts for Your Promise
✍️

"Going forward, I commit to showing up for you by..."

✍️

"One thing I will do differently from this week forward is..."

✍️

"I choose you — and here is what that choice looks like in action..."
