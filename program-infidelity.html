Infidelity Recovery

A carefully structured 180-day path through betrayal, grief, and rebuilding. This program holds space for both partners — guiding you through accountability, understanding, forgiveness, and the slow, real work of renewed commitment.

First 7 Days

Where the program begins

Every mission in the Infidelity Recovery program is designed with care — no rushing, no glossing over. Here is a preview of the first week.

Day 1
🔦

Name the Truth

"Healing begins when you stop talking around what happened and say it plainly."

Sit together without distractions. The betraying partner speaks the facts of what happened — not to justify, but to acknowledge. The other partner listens without interrupting. This is not a debate. It is the first act of honesty.

⏱ 20–30 min 3 guided prompts included
Day 2
🖤

The Grief Room

"Grief belongs here. Give it space before you try to fix anything."

The betrayed partner speaks about what has been lost — safety, the version of your partner you believed in, the future you imagined. The betraying partner witnesses without defending. Then switch. Both partners share what they are grieving.

⏱ 25–35 min 3 guided prompts included
Day 3
🪞

Full Accountability

"Accountability without minimizing is what makes repair possible."

The betraying partner writes and reads a full accountability statement — an honest reckoning with the harm caused. No "but" statements. No reasons that excuse. Just: I did this. I see what it did to you. I take responsibility.

⏱ 30 min 3 guided prompts included
Day 4
🌀

The Why Conversation

"Understanding why is not forgiveness — it is the map back to each other."

This is not about excusing what happened. It is about understanding the conditions that made the betrayal possible. Both partners share perspectives — what was missing, what went unspoken, what each person was carrying alone.

⏱ 30–40 min 3 guided prompts included
Day 5
🌿

The Forgiveness Question

"Forgiveness is a direction, not a destination you arrive at today."

Neither partner is required to feel forgiving right now. The exercise is to explore — out loud, together — what forgiveness means to each of you. What would it look like? What would need to happen first? You are not being asked to rush.

⏱ 20–25 min 3 guided prompts included
Day 6
🧱

Rebuilding Together

"You are not rebuilding what was — you are building something that never existed before."

Together, name five concrete commitments you are each making to the rebuilt relationship. Not promises to never hurt each other — but specific, behavioral, observable commitments. Write them down. Read them to each other.

⏱ 25–30 min 3 guided prompts included
Day 7
🌅

One Day Forward

"The work is long. Today you simply choose to continue."

Reflect on the first six days together. What has surfaced? What has shifted? Each partner names one thing they are proud of themselves for — and one thing they are grateful for in the other. Then make a shared commitment to return tomorrow.

⏱ 20 min 3 guided prompts included
